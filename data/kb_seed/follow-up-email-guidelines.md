# Follow-Up Email Guidelines

The best follow-up email is short, specific, and easy to forward.

Recommended structure:

- Thank the participants and reference the meeting purpose.
- Summarize the top pains or goals in the customer's own language.
- List the decisions made during the call.
- Capture open questions that still need answers.
- Confirm the next meeting or action with date and owner.

Example topics worth including:

- The manual step currently slowing down the team.
- The metric the buyer wants to improve.
- The internal approval step needed before purchase.

Avoid:

- Generic "just checking in" language.
- Rewriting the entire call transcript.
- Leaving out the agreed next step.

A useful follow-up email should help the champion repeat the case internally without joining another meeting first.
