# Discovery Call Checklist

Purpose: guide a first sales conversation toward business pain, urgency, and next steps.

Use this checklist during an early discovery call:

- Open with the meeting goal and confirm the available time.
- Ask what changed recently that made the prospect take the meeting.
- Clarify the current process, owner, and tools already in use.
- Quantify the cost of the problem in time, revenue, or conversion loss.
- Identify who is affected when the problem is not solved.
- Ask how success will be measured after a rollout.
- Confirm budget process and buying timeline.
- Map the decision makers, champions, and blockers.
- End with a clear recap and one committed next action.

Signals of a strong discovery call:

- The rep can state the customer's top pain in one sentence.
- The customer confirms business impact instead of only describing features.
- Timeline and decision process are explicit.
- The next meeting has a concrete purpose.

Common mistakes:

- Demoing too early before pain is clear.
- Accepting vague answers such as "efficiency" without examples.
- Ending without owner, date, or mutual commitment.
