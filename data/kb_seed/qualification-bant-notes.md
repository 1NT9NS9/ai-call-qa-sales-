# Qualification Notes Using BANT

BANT is a lightweight qualification framework covering budget, authority, need, and timing.

Budget:

- Confirm whether funding exists, not only whether the buyer likes the solution.
- Identify whether the deal needs a new budget request.

Authority:

- Separate the daily user, champion, evaluator, and final approver.
- Ask who else must agree before a purchase can happen.

Need:

- Document the business problem, current workaround, and measurable impact.
- Confirm why the current approach is no longer acceptable.

Timing:

- Anchor to a business event such as hiring growth, quarter-end targets, or system replacement.
- Test whether the stated timeline is real by asking what happens if the project slips.

BANT should not be treated as a rigid script. Its purpose is to reveal whether the deal is real and what evidence is still missing.
