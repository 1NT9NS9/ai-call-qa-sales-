# Pricing Objection Handling

When a prospect says the product is expensive, the rep should not defend price immediately. The first move is to understand the concern.

Recommended flow:

1. Acknowledge the concern without becoming defensive.
2. Ask whether the issue is total budget, timing, approval, or perceived value.
3. Reconnect price to the business problem and expected return.
4. Compare the cost of inaction to the cost of the solution.
5. Confirm whether price is the real blocker or a proxy for another concern.

Useful questions:

- Which part of the proposal feels hardest to justify internally?
- What budget range had you expected?
- If the team solves this problem this quarter, what is that worth?
- Besides price, what would stop you from moving forward?

Poor responses to avoid:

- Immediate discounting before value is established.
- Long feature lists that ignore the actual concern.
- Pushing for close before approval steps are clear.

A pricing objection is often a value, priority, or risk objection in disguise.
