# Demo Call Best Practices

A demo should prove relevance, not showcase every feature.

Before the demo:

- Restate the prospect's pain points from the prior conversation.
- Confirm the audience and their roles.
- Agree on what the demo must answer before the meeting ends.

During the demo:

- Show the workflow that maps directly to the buyer's stated problem.
- Use the prospect's language instead of product jargon.
- Pause often to confirm whether the example matches their reality.
- Highlight measurable outcomes, not only interface details.
- Track objections and address them in context.

After the demo:

- Recap the pains discussed and the capability that addressed each one.
- Confirm unresolved risks, integrations, or approval questions.
- Set the next step while all stakeholders are present if possible.

Warning signs:

- The rep talks for most of the meeting.
- The buyer asks basic "what problem does this solve?" questions late in the call.
- The meeting ends with "we will get back to you" and no date.
